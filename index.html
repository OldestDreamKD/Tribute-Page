Wanda Maximoff

"Haunted by loss, powerful but conflicted hero."

History

  • Captain America: The Winter Soldier (Post-Credits Scene - 2014)
    • Introduction: A brief glimpse into Wanda and Pietro Maximoff's backstory is shown. They are depicted as S.H.I.E.L.D. test subjects who underwent experiments with Loki's Scepter, which housed the Mind Stone. The Scepter amplified their natural abilities, granting Wanda telekinesis and energy manipulation.
    • Motivation: Initially hostile, Wanda appears alongside her brother Pietro, working for Baron Strucker, a Hydra scientist who utilized the Scepter for his experiments.
  • Avengers: Age of Ultron (2015)
    • Alignment Shift: After learning of Hydra's true intentions and Ultron's plan to eradicate humanity, Wanda and Pietro defect and join the Avengers to stop him.
    • Power Development: We see a wider range of Wanda's abilities, including telekinesis, telepathy, and energy blasts.
    • Loss and Grief: Witnessing Pietro's sacrifice to save Captain America deeply impacts Wanda.
  • Captain America: Civil War (2016)
    • Internal Conflict:Wanda is placed under house arrest by the government due to the destruction caused during the Battle of Sokovia. She grapples with the weight of her actions and the grief from Pietro's death.
    • Team Choice: Despite her reservations, Wanda sides with Captain America's team, who believe superheroes shouldn't be controlled by governments. This decision leads to her becoming a fugitive.
  • Avengers: Infinity War (2018)
    • Desperate Measures:Thanos arrives on Earth seeking the Mind Stone, now embedded in Vision's forehead. Wanda is determined to protect Vision, the man she loves.
    • Power Growth: Wanda demonstrates a significant increase in power, holding her own against Thanos and even briefly destroying the Mind Stone to prevent him from acquiring it.
    • Heartbreaking Decision: In a tearful goodbye, Wanda destroys the Mind Stone, killing Vision in the process.
  • Avengers: Endgame (2019)
    • Grief and Resolve: Still mourning Vision's death, Wanda joins the Avengers in their time travel mission to undo Thanos' snap.
    • Vengeance and Power: During the final battle against Thanos, Wanda unleashes her full power, overwhelming him with a barrage of energy blasts. However, she is ultimately unable to defeat him alone.
  • WandaVision (2021 - Miniseries)
    • Emotional Trauma: This series delves deeper into Wanda's emotional state following the events of Endgame. To cope with her grief, she unknowingly creates an alternate reality in a town called Westview, where she lives a seemingly perfect suburban life with Vision.
    • Reality Warping: The series explores the extent of Wanda's reality-warping abilities, showcasing her power to manipulate reality itself.
    • Confronting Reality:Wanda is forced to confront the consequences of her actions and dismantle the Westview anomaly, releasing the town and its people but also letting go of her idyllic fantasy world.
    • Acceptance and Growth: The ending suggests Wanda is finally beginning to accept her grief and the immense power she wields.
  • Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness (2022)
    • Corrupted by Grief:Influenced by the Darkhold, a powerful book of forbidden magic, Wanda becomes corrupted by her grief for Vision and her lost children in the Westview reality.
    • Antagonistic Role: She seeks to use the power of America Chavez, who can travel the multiverse, to find a universe where she can be with her children. This puts her at odds with Doctor Strange and America Chavez.
    • Moral Conflict:Despite her villainous actions, the film portrays Wanda as a complex character struggling with immense emotional pain. There are moments where she questions her choices and seems conflicted.
    • Sacrifice and Redemption: In the end, Wanda realizes the harm she has caused and chooses to destroy all copies of the Darkhold across the multiverse, sacrificing herself in the process.
